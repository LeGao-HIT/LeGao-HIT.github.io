I am a Ph.D. candidate at Harbin Institute of Technology in Integrated Circuit Science and Engineering. My research focuses on reliability prediction and remaining useful life (RUL) estimation for power electronic devices, with an emphasis on physics-informed machine learning (PIML), sparse-data prognostics, uncertainty-aware modeling, and device-health monitoring.

My work combines accelerated degradation experiments, space-environment irradiation testing, physical degradation modeling, and deep learning methods for power MOSFETs and related electronic components. I have designed and built power-cycling and real-time device-health monitoring systems, and I use Python, MATLAB, LabVIEW, Saber, and LaTeX in my research workflow.

I am interested in robust RUL prediction under limited degradation data, physics-guided neural networks for reliability modeling, uncertainty quantification, and reliability assurance for power electronic devices used in harsh environments.
